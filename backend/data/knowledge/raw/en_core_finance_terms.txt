The price-to-earnings ratio, also called the PE ratio, compares a company's share price with its earnings per share and is often used as a shorthand measure of valuation. Revenue refers to the gross inflow a company earns from selling goods or providing services before most costs are deducted. Net income, or net profit, measures what remains after operating costs, interest, taxes, and other gains or losses are recognized. Revenue shows business scale, while net income shows bottom-line profitability. A company can grow revenue while net income declines if margins weaken, operating expenses rise, or one-off charges increase.
